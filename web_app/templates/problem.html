Detecting Fraud on the Secondary Event Market

Fraud Prediction

Our team of expert data scientists took on the task of predicting fraud for a new e-commerce site that provides online hosting for events. Not every hosting request is legitimate. Our task was to utilize machine learning algorithms to better predict fraud from incoming requests.

Exploratory Data Analysis

With 45 different features to predict on, it was quickly understood that we needed to narrow this down to the most influential columns. It was believed that the numerical columns would be the most influential so we began running simple analysis on just these values.

A few important features dominated our EDA and we decided to move forward with a list of the top 7. One of the more intuitive features was age of user (how long this user has been with registered with the company). Below we graphed the percentage of fraud over the age of the user.

As depicted above, the majority of our cases of fraud occur with the most recently created accounts. This makes sense, as these accounts are typically created for the sole purpose of making fraudulent events. The spikes shown with higher user age are an indication that those user accounts could have been hacked. These older cases of fraud will be harder to predict and rely more heavily on other features.

Natural Language Processing

The obvious non-numerical column to use in our predictions was the description. Fraudulent events will most likely have information here which makes them easily identifiable as fraud. This may be the use of all capitol letters, certain key phrases, or even a non-sensical description. Utilizing natural language processing we began running models on this feature to pull out certain patterns to help predict fraud along-side the numerical features.

Model
