At the 2012 Sloan Sports Analytics Conference, Muthu Alagappan famously posited that there are 13 NBA positions, as opposed to the traditional 5.

His presentation certainly wasn't without merit, but its findings fell short in a number of ways. The New York Times elaborated on a couple of these issues at the time.

In spite of these reservations regarding his conclusions, I immediately saw the value in Alagappan's project, and in attacking the problem he had set forth to solve.

My goal in this project was in two parts:
 a) Redefine the NBA positions using feature extraction, dimension reduction, and clustering methods
 b) Extract pattern(s) by regressing on player-type distributions, in an attempt to find some sort of 'winning formula'.
